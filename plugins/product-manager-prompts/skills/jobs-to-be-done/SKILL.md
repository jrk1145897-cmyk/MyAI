# Jobs-to-be-Done Analysis Skill

Helps product managers explore and document customer needs using the Jobs-to-be-Done framework, influenced by Osterwalder's Value Proposition Canvas.

## When to Use

- Understanding customer motivations before building features
- Validating product-market fit
- Discovering unmet needs
- Creating empathy maps for personas
- Refining value propositions

## How to Apply

When the user provides context about a customer segment or asks for JTBD analysis:

1. **Gather Context First**: Ensure you understand the target customer segment, their behaviors, preferences, and current solutions
2. **Apply the Template**: Walk through each section systematically
3. **Ask Probing Questions**: If context is missing, ask the user to provide more detail
4. **Output as Markdown**: Render the completed analysis in a code block for easy copying

## Jobs-to-be-Done Template

### 1. Customer Jobs

Identify the jobs your customers are trying to get done:

#### Functional Jobs
- [Tasks customers need to perform to accomplish practical goals]
- [Concrete actions they take to solve problems]
- [Workflows they complete regularly]

#### Social Jobs
- [How customers want to be perceived by others]
- [Status or recognition they seek]
- [Professional image they want to project]

#### Emotional Jobs
- [Emotional states customers seek to achieve]
- [Feelings they want to avoid]
- [Sense of security, confidence, or satisfaction they desire]

### 2. Pains

Describe the pains customers experience:

#### Challenges
- [Obstacles customers face in getting jobs done]
- [Frustrations with current solutions]
- [Barriers that slow them down]

#### Costliness
- [What costs too much time]
- [What costs too much money]
- [What requires too much effort]

#### Common Mistakes
- [Frequent errors customers make]
- [Misunderstandings that lead to problems]
- [Pitfalls that could be prevented]

#### Unresolved Problems
- [Issues current solutions don't address]
- [Gaps in existing offerings]
- [Workarounds customers have created]

### 3. Gains

Detail the gains customers seek:

#### Expectations
- [What could exceed current solution performance]
- [Features that would delight them]
- [Quality improvements they'd value]

#### Savings
- [Time savings that would delight them]
- [Cost savings they'd appreciate]
- [Effort reduction they'd value]

#### Adoption Factors
- [What would increase likelihood of trying a solution]
- [Trust signals they need]
- [Risk reducers that matter]

#### Life Improvement
- [How a solution could make work easier]
- [Quality of life improvements]
- [Professional growth enablement]

## Validation Questions

When reviewing existing JTBD work, check:

1. **Completeness**: Are all three job types covered (functional, social, emotional)?
2. **Customer Voice**: Is this written from the customer's perspective, not the company's?
3. **Specificity**: Are the jobs concrete and observable, not abstract?
4. **Pain Severity**: Are pains ranked or prioritized by intensity?
5. **Gain Relevance**: Do the gains directly address the stated pains?
6. **Evidence**: Is there customer research backing these claims?
