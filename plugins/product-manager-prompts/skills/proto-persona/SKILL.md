# Proto-Persona Skill

Helps product managers create initial proto-persona profiles that can be validated and refined through research. A proto-persona is an educated hypothesis about who your users are.

## When to Use

- Starting a new product with limited user research
- Creating alignment on target users before deep research
- Preparing for user interviews and validation
- Documenting assumptions about user needs
- Kickstarting persona development workshops

## Proto-Persona vs Validated Persona

**Proto-Persona**: Based on team assumptions, market knowledge, and limited data. Used to guide initial research.

**Validated Persona**: Based on actual user research, interviews, and data. Used to guide product decisions.

Always treat proto-personas as hypotheses to validate, not facts to assume.

## Proto-Persona Canvas Template

```markdown
## Proto Persona Canvas

### Name
[Alliterative name that's memorable, e.g., "Marketing Maya", "Developer Dan"]

### Bio & Demographics
- **Age**: [Range or specific]
- **Geography**: [Location, urban/rural]
- **Social Status**: [Income level, family situation]
- **Online Presence**: [Social media usage, tech savviness]
- **Partner/Family Status**: [Single, married, kids]
- **Leisure Activities**: [Hobbies, interests]
- **Career Status**: [Job title, years of experience, industry]
- **Education**: [Level, field]

### Quotes
[Multiple quotes that capture what they say, feel, and think]
- "[Quote about their frustrations]"
- "[Quote about their aspirations]"
- "[Quote about their work/life]"

### Pains
[Pain points related to the problem space]
- [Pain 1: frustration or challenge]
- [Pain 2: time/money/effort cost]
- [Pain 3: current workaround hassles]

### What is This Person Trying to Accomplish?
[Observable behaviors and jobs-to-be-done]
- [Task 1 they perform regularly]
- [Task 2 they're trying to complete]
- [Behavior we observe in their workflow]

### Goals
[Wants, needs, and dreams]
- **Short-term**: [Immediate goals]
- **Long-term**: [Career/life aspirations]
- **Underlying Need**: [Deeper motivation]

### Attitudes & Influences

**Decision Making Authority**
[Does this person have authority to buy? Are they an influencer, decision maker, or end user?]

**Decision Influencers**
[Who influences their decisions? Boss, peers, family, online reviews, industry experts?]

**Beliefs & Attitudes**
- [Belief 1 that affects their decisions]
- [Belief 2 about technology/solutions]
- [Attitude toward change/new tools]
```

## Validation Priorities

After creating a proto-persona, prioritize validating:

### High Priority (Interview Immediately)
1. **Pains**: Are these real frustrations they experience?
2. **Goals**: Are these their actual priorities?
3. **Decision Authority**: Can they actually buy/adopt?

### Medium Priority (Observe/Survey)
1. **Tasks**: Do they actually do these things?
2. **Quotes**: Would they say something like this?
3. **Influences**: Who actually affects their decisions?

### Low Priority (Refine Over Time)
1. **Demographics**: Are these roughly accurate?
2. **Attitudes**: How do they actually think?
3. **Name/Bio**: Does this resonate with real users?

## Validation Questions for User Interviews

Use these questions to validate proto-persona assumptions:

### About Their Role
- "Walk me through a typical day/week for you."
- "What takes up most of your time?"
- "What do you wish you could spend more time on?"

### About Their Pains
- "What's the most frustrating part of [task]?"
- "What have you tried to solve [problem]?"
- "How much time/money does [pain] cost you?"

### About Their Goals
- "What would success look like for you?"
- "What's standing in the way of [goal]?"
- "If you could wave a magic wand, what would change?"

### About Decision Making
- "How do you typically evaluate new tools/solutions?"
- "Who else is involved in decisions like this?"
- "What would make you hesitant to try something new?"

## Common Mistakes

- **Designing for Yourself**: The persona looks suspiciously like the PM
- **Too Generic**: "Busy professional who wants to save time"
- **Too Aspirational**: Describing ideal customers, not real ones
- **Feature-Driven**: Defining users by what they'd want, not who they are
- **Static Thinking**: Never updating personas after validation
- **Single Persona**: Assuming all users are the same

---

## Workshop Facilitation Tips

When creating proto-personas with a team:

1. **Start with Data**: Share any existing research, support tickets, sales notes
2. **Limit to 3-4**: Don't create too many personas
3. **Name Debates**: Agreeing on names forces alignment on who they are
4. **Quote Writing**: If you can't imagine their quotes, you don't know them
5. **Pains Before Goals**: Understand problems before aspirations
6. **Mark Assumptions**: Explicitly flag what needs validation
