# Customer Journey Mapping Skill

Helps product managers create comprehensive customer journey maps to understand customer experiences, identify pain points, and align teams on improvement opportunities.

## When to Use

- Understanding the full customer experience
- Identifying friction points in the buyer journey
- Aligning cross-functional teams on customer touchpoints
- Planning improvements across the customer lifecycle
- Preparing for customer experience redesigns

## Prerequisites

Before creating a journey map, gather:

1. **Key Stakeholders**: Representatives from marketing, sales, customer service
2. **Buyer Personas**: Detailed profiles including demographics, psychographics, goals
3. **Journey Stages**: Awareness → Consideration → Decision → Service → Loyalty
4. **Touchpoints**: All places customers interact with your brand

## Customer Journey Map Template

```markdown
## Buyer Journey Map

### Persona
**Name**: [Persona name]
**Segment**: [Target segment]
**Key Characteristics**: [2-3 defining traits]

### Journey Stages

| Stage | Awareness | Consideration | Decision | Service | Loyalty |
|-------|-----------|---------------|----------|---------|---------|
| **Customer Actions** | [Actions to become aware] | [Evaluation behaviors] | [Purchase steps] | [Post-purchase actions] | [Repeat/referral behaviors] |
| **Customer Goals** | [Learning/curiosity goals] | [Finding right fit] | [Completing transaction] | [Getting value] | [Long-term satisfaction] |
| **Touchpoints** | [Media, social, word of mouth] | [Website, sales, reviews] | [Checkout, contract] | [Support, documentation] | [Community, rewards] |
| **Customer Experience** | [Emotions: interest, confusion] | [Emotions: excitement, overwhelm] | [Emotions: anxiety, satisfaction] | [Emotions: frustration, relief] | [Emotions: loyalty, advocacy] |
| **KPIs** | [Reach, awareness, engagement] | [Traffic, time on site, leads] | [Conversion, order value] | [CSAT, NPS, resolution time] | [CLV, retention, referral rate] |
| **Business Goals** | [Brand awareness, traffic] | [Lead quality, engagement] | [Conversion optimization] | [Customer satisfaction] | [Retention, referrals] |
| **Teams Involved** | [Marketing, comms] | [Marketing, sales, web] | [Sales, marketing, CS] | [Customer service/success] | [CS, marketing, product] |
```

## Three-Path Journey Simulator

For deeper analysis, map Happy, Fail, and Difficult paths:

```markdown
| Stage | Awareness | Consideration | Decision | Delivery & Use | Loyalty & Advocacy |
|-------|-----------|---------------|----------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Customer Activities** | [Actions to become aware] | [Research behaviors] | [Decision steps] | [Usage actions] | [Post-use behaviors] |
| **Customer Goals** | [Learning goals] | [Fit-finding goals] | [Intent to act] | [Value realization] | [Long-term goals] |
| **Happy Path** | Excited, curious | Confident, engaged | Assured, ready | Delighted, empowered | Loyal, advocating |
| **Fail Path** | Confused, disconnected | Overwhelmed, lost | Regretful, dropped | Frustrated, stuck | Disappointed, churned |
| **Difficult Path** | Skeptical, doubtful | Conflicted, uncertain | Stressed, hesitant | Needs extra support | Mixed feelings, passive |
| **Business Goal** | [Awareness metric] | [Engagement metric] | [Conversion metric] | [Satisfaction metric] | [Advocacy metric] |
```

## Journey Mapping Process

### Step 1: Set Objectives
- Define primary goals for creating the journey map
- Identify areas for improvement, customer motivations, or team alignment needs

### Step 2: Know Your Customers
- Gather data through surveys, user testing, support interactions, analytics
- Understand needs, preferences, and emotions at each stage

### Step 3: Choose a Persona
- Select ONE buyer persona per journey map
- Create separate maps for different personas to avoid confusion

### Step 4: Map Touchpoints to Stages
- Identify ALL ways customers interact with your brand at each stage
- Include positive touchpoints (encourage action) and negative (roadblocks)

### Step 5: Visualize the Map
- Organize findings into visual format (spreadsheet, flowchart)
- Include stages, actions, emotions, touchpoints, opportunities

### Step 6: Test and Refine
- Analyze for pain points, bottlenecks, improvement areas
- Brainstorm solutions, prioritize by impact and feasibility
- Update regularly as customer needs and strategies evolve

## Validation Checklist

When reviewing journey maps, verify:

- [ ] **All Stages Covered**: From awareness through loyalty/advocacy
- [ ] **Customer Perspective**: Written from customer's view, not company's
- [ ] **Emotional Journey**: Includes feelings at each stage
- [ ] **Touchpoint Completeness**: All interaction points identified
- [ ] **Cross-Team Input**: Multiple departments contributed
- [ ] **Data Backed**: Based on real customer research, not assumptions
- [ ] **Actionable Insights**: Identifies specific improvement opportunities
- [ ] **KPI Alignment**: Metrics defined for each stage

## Follow-Up Actions

After completing a journey map:

1. **Identify Quick Wins**: What can be fixed in the next sprint?
2. **Find Fail Path Triggers**: What causes customers to drop off?
3. **Add Ownership**: Who owns each touchpoint?
4. **Define Interventions**: What can prevent difficult paths?
5. **Set Metrics**: How will you measure improvements?
