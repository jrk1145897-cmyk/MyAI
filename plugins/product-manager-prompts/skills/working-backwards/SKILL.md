# Working Backwards Skill

Helps product managers create vision documents using Amazon's Working Backwards methodology: writing the press release and FAQ before building the product.

## When to Use

- Defining product vision before development begins
- Aligning stakeholders on what success looks like
- Testing whether a product idea is compelling
- Creating clarity about customer value
- Preparing for executive reviews

## The Working Backwards Philosophy

Start at the end: write the announcement as if the product already exists. This forces clarity about:
- Who cares about this product?
- What problem does it solve?
- Why is it better than alternatives?
- How will customers react?

## Visionary Press Release Template

Write the announcement you'd want to publish when the product ships:

```markdown
## Press Release

### Headline
"[Product/Service Name] by [Company] Aims to [Main Purpose/Goal]"

### Dateline
[City], [State/Province], [Country], [Date] —

### Introduction Paragraph
Today, [Company Name], a [type of organization], announced [key news item], a [brief description]. This [development/product] is set to [main benefit or goal], addressing [key issue or need].

### Second Paragraph
[Product Name] will [describe what it does or solves]. "[Insert quote from a key executive]," said [Name], [Title]. This initiative reflects [Company's] commitment to [core value or mission].

### Additional Details Paragraph
In addition to [mentioned features], [Product Name] also [additional features or benefits]. According to [source or statistic], [relevant data supporting the news].

### Boilerplate
[Company Name], founded in [year], is a [type of company] known for [main products/services]. With a focus on [company mission or values], [Company Name] has [achievements or milestones].

### Call to Action
For more information about [Product Name], visit [website] or contact [Media Contact Name] at [contact information].

### Media Contact
**[Contact Name]**
Title: [Title]
Phone: [Phone]
Email: [Email]
```

## Futuristic FAQ Template

Answer questions stakeholders and customers will ask:

```markdown
## Product FAQ

### 1. General Information

**Q1: What is [Product/Service]?**
A1: [Brief, clear description]

**Q2: Why was [Product/Service] developed?**
A2: [Purpose and motivation]

**Q3: How can I stay updated?**
A3: [Newsletters, updates, channels]

### 2. Key Stakeholders

**Q1: How does this align with company strategy?**
A1: [Strategic fit explanation]

**Q2: What are the expected outcomes?**
A2: [Anticipated benefits for the company]

**Q3: How can stakeholders contribute?**
A3: [Feedback and involvement channels]

### 3. Potential Customers

**Q1: How does this address my needs?**
A1: [Problem-solution fit]

**Q2: What makes this different from competitors?**
A2: [Unique selling points]

**Q3: How can I try it?**
A3: [Trials, demos, resources]

### 4. Sales & Marketing

**Q1: What are the key selling points?**
A1: [Main features and benefits to emphasize]

**Q2: Who is the target audience?**
A2: [Target description and approach strategy]

**Q3: What marketing resources exist?**
A3: [Available materials and access]

### 5. Engineering

**Q1: What were the major technical challenges?**
A1: [Technical hurdles and solutions]

**Q2: How does this incorporate innovation?**
A2: [Technological advancements involved]

**Q3: Is there ongoing technical support?**
A3: [Support structures and development plans]

### 6. Product Owners

**Q1: What is the long-term roadmap?**
A1: [Future vision and roadmap]

**Q2: How will feedback be implemented?**
A2: [Feedback integration process]

**Q3: How is success measured?**
A3: [Metrics and KPIs]
```

## Product Context Checklist

Before writing PR/FAQ, gather these 7 elements:

1. **Nature of Product/Service**: What is it?
2. **Target Persona**: Who is it for?
3. **Key Features/USPs**: What makes it special?
4. **Company Context**: Mission, values, background
5. **Goals/Challenges Addressed**: What problems does it solve?
6. **Supporting Data**: Research, articles, artifacts
7. **Target Audiences**: Who needs to know about this?

## Validation Questions

When reviewing Working Backwards documents:

### Press Release
- [ ] **Headline Compelling**: Would a reporter want to cover this?
- [ ] **Customer Focus**: Does it emphasize customer benefit over features?
- [ ] **Believable Quote**: Is the executive quote authentic and specific?
- [ ] **Clear Value**: Can a stranger understand why this matters?
- [ ] **Differentiation**: Does it explain what's new/different?

### FAQ
- [ ] **All Audiences Covered**: Stakeholders, customers, sales, engineering, product?
- [ ] **Tough Questions Addressed**: Does it answer skeptics' concerns?
- [ ] **Consistent Messaging**: Do answers align with press release?
- [ ] **Specific Answers**: Are responses concrete, not vague?
- [ ] **Roadmap Clarity**: Is the future direction clear?

---

## Common Pitfalls

- **Feature Lists**: Don't make the press release a feature dump
- **Jargon**: Write for external audiences, not internal teams
- **Vague Benefits**: "Improved productivity" means nothing without specifics
- **Missing Differentiation**: If it sounds like competitors, it won't get coverage
- **Ignored Skeptics**: The FAQ must address "Why should I care?"
